UX DESIGN

Problem Statement

The cannabis industry is still very new given the recent legalization of marijuana in California. Given the modernity of the market, there is a lack of widespread information and education of the how’s and what’s of cannabis sales.

Our Solution

A mobile app that would allow business and users to learn more and catch up on up and coming news.

My Role

I was one of the later members to join a remote team of other UX designers and built a prototype to show stakeholders. Over the course of a month, I helped by creating wireframes.

Tools: Adobe Illustrator

Skills: wireframing, prototyping

Background

64POS’s inception came about as a way to capitalize on the incoming cannabis market. Their previous research showed that the market would hold a large interest in the young demographic and would thus need designing that would appeal to that group. They wanted to create an aggregation site to make navigation in the industry much easier for share for customers, businesspeople, and stakeholders. I was given the opportunity to design a part of the site in the span of two months.

The Work

Needfinding:
We prepared a competitive analysis chart which included features of many different news apps. The inspiration was to create an app that was familiar to people so user navigation would not be complicated. As a team, we went over the chart sheet together and discussed which attributes were necessary and which were superfluous.

Prototyping:
Once we established the key features, we dove into ideation and started conceptualizing. I helped to create wireframes that would be the screens of the mobile app. Once we finished our delegated screens, our team lead stitched the frames together to make an interactive interface.

I kept the UI similar to many other news apps for a sense of familiarity for users that frequently browse news apps.

Presentation with Stakeholders

Presenting the current prototype with our stakeholders garnered a lot of praise for our team. Our team lead was able to present the work along with the CEO to the stakeholders and won them over.

Afterthoughts

This was my first time working in an industry startup and it gave me more of a hands-on experience of what UI and UX designers did from day to day. I am thankful to have the opportunity and am glad that the stakeholders liked our sketches and wireframes enough to give us the green light for the next step.
