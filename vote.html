CAUSE

Students at UC San Diego have many great characteristics but they lack political engagement. UC San Diego is known for its lack of engagement in political elections, whether school-wide or national. Among many of non-voting demographics, many students are interested in engaging but are often lost on the process due to the overwhelming amount of information. We chose to focus on helping them be more knowledgeable on the voting process so that they can feel confident to vote.

HOW WE HELP

We created an app, Vote, for our social campaign to provide an accessible resource for relevant election information . We hoped to better educate and inform students on campus so they could be more active in civic affairs.

RESEARCH AND KEY INSIGHTS

For our research, we did some field study supplemented with statistical data. Marne worked as a poll worker for the past elections and was able to observe the voting experience through the student‚Äôs perspective. From her experience, there were a few key insights

1. Many students were often flustered and unprepared when approaching polls because they were not sure what requirements there were for voting.

2. Students do not fully understand the different voting types which leads to invalid voting.

3. Students ineligible to vote do not feel like there is a point in being involved, contributing to general apathy.

OUR IDEA

Based on what students experienced, we wanted to tackle their issues with something relevant and accessible. We were at first interested in social media as that is the most reliable platform most people have access to. We had ideas like a political Tinder app where people would swipe to learn more about each other‚Äôs political views and sides or a current news Instagram account with relevant content. Ultimately, we decided to have a voting guide app catered towards UC San Diego students because we felt it would be the best way to inform and engage the student population.

Since people today go to technology to find their solutions, we thought it would be fitting to have relevant voter information at the tap of their phones.

SKETCHES

Amberly and I created some sketches on different possible interfaces of the app. Some of the focuses were on navigating while others were on content. In the end, we combined our ideas to create a more simple layout as to not overwhelm users.

FINAL DESIGNS
USED IN CONTEXT

The app is to be used before and during an actual election season, which would be the most appropriate time for UC San Diego students to be informed on how to be best prepare. By providing an accessible source of education on the entire process, the app helps to foster confidence. The app works to break down the voting process--from registering to vote, understanding the ballot, and to knowing how to vote on election day--into manageable steps. The app purposefully separates the voting process into different components to create a simpler and more easily digestible resource for beginners.
