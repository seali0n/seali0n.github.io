BRANDING | VISUAL DESIGN

TOOLS
Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator
MY ROLE
Graphic + Visual Designer

ABOUT EARTHAPPS

EarthApps is a hackathon event focused on sustainability and environmental related issues. The hackathon is open to all high school and college students in St. Louis, Michigan and is run by Clean My Planet. The goal of the event is to inspire youth to think critically of environmental issues and work in teams to problem solve for potential solutions.

The planning team needed a strong brand presence that would be appealing to a younger audience and have an open inviting presence for a younger demographic. As the Visual Designer, I worked with the team to tackle this challenge. This project is still a work in process and will be updated regularly!

BRANDING PROCESS

I started off the branding process but getting to know the team, the org's values, and event's goals. I had a brainstorming session with the team to explore how we wanted our brand represented. From our session, we discovered terms that we wanted to resonate with our branding. High school and college students are the targeted audience for the event. As such, we wanted to create a youthful look that would be inviting to a younger crowd, especially for those who may not have as much experience in this field. The main focus of the branding should be fun, inviting, and accessible. I worked on creating moodboards and some style tiles that conveyed our branding adjectives along with an environmental theme.

After discussion with the team, we agreed to combine aspects of both style tiles to create another iteration.

LOGO DESIGN

With the established branding in hand, I moved on to focus on the logo. Since the event is not focused on any specific environmental issue, I wanted to create something that could convey several themes. I thought it would be fitting to have a logo that was a hybrid of different issues. With that as my main inspiration, I went to create sketches and translate them into digital graphics.

Looking over my draft of logos, I realized that some of them may look awkward at different sizes, particularly the ones with an overhang or with unbalanced white space. Because of this, I focused more on the logos that were more balanced visually as they would scale better.

Narrowing down the designs to two choices, I brought the drafts to the team. The team liked the rain drop design more in the end and I opted for a non-stroke logo to bring more focus to the design.

NEXT STEPS

As the timeline of the COVID-19 pandemic is still uncertain, the EarthApps team has collectively agreed to hold off any further planning until further notice. As we learn more, the next steps would be to start working on a website as a resource point for information of the design challenge event.
